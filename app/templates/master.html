Disaster response is one of the most important problems that we're trying to solve in Data Science and Machine Learning right now.

In the wake of a disaster there are millions of communications either direct or via social media, right at the time when disaster response organizations have the least capacity to filter and pull out the messages which are the most important. Furthermore, only one in every thousand messages is actually relevant to the disaster response professionals, according to Robert Munro, former CTO of Figure Eight (now Appen).

Machine learning to the rescue

Disasters are typically responded to by different organizations that take care of different parts of the problem. So one organization will care about water, another will care about blocked roads, another will care about medical supplies. But sorting through help requests by keywords only is ineffective. For example, when you look at a keyword like "water", that's actually a very small percent of the time that it will map to someone who needs fresh drinking wate. Moreover, that will miss miss people who say they're thirsty, but don't use the word "water."

Thanks to Figure Eight's effort of combining messages from several real-life distasters and consistently labeling them, we can train a supervised machine learning model and ensure that each organization gets relevant help requests.
